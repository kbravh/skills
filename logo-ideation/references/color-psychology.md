# Color Psychology Reference

## Primary Colors

### Blue
**Associations**: Trust, reliability, stability, professionalism, calm, security
**Industries**: Finance, technology, healthcare, corporate, insurance, social media
**Variations**:
- Navy: Authority, traditional
- Royal blue: Confidence, dependability
- Light blue: Calm, approachable, fresh

### Red
**Associations**: Energy, passion, urgency, excitement, appetite, power
**Industries**: Food & beverage, entertainment, retail, automotive, sports
**Cautions**: Can signal danger or aggression; use strategically

### Yellow
**Associations**: Optimism, clarity, warmth, creativity, attention-grabbing
**Industries**: Consumer products, children's brands, food, entertainment
**Cautions**: Can cause eye fatigue; pair with darker colors

### Green
**Associations**: Nature, growth, health, wealth, freshness, sustainability
**Industries**: Environmental, health & wellness, finance, organic products
**Variations**:
- Dark green: Wealth, prestige
- Bright green: Energy, freshness
- Sage/muted: Calm, natural

## Secondary Colors

### Purple
**Associations**: Creativity, luxury, wisdom, spirituality, imagination
**Industries**: Beauty, creative services, luxury goods, spirituality
**Variations**:
- Deep purple: Royalty, luxury
- Lavender: Feminine, soft, wellness

### Orange
**Associations**: Enthusiasm, creativity, friendliness, confidence, adventure
**Industries**: Youth brands, food, sports, technology startups
**Notes**: Combines energy of red with friendliness of yellow

### Pink
**Associations**: Playfulness, romance, compassion, nurturing
**Industries**: Beauty, fashion, children's products, dating
**Variations**:
- Hot pink: Bold, energetic, youthful
- Blush: Sophisticated, modern, gender-neutral when muted

## Neutrals

### Black
**Associations**: Sophistication, luxury, power, elegance, timelessness
**Industries**: Fashion, luxury goods, technology, automotive
**Notes**: Creates strong contrast; works with any accent color

### White
**Associations**: Purity, simplicity, cleanliness, minimalism, space
**Industries**: Healthcare, technology, luxury, lifestyle
**Notes**: Essential for breathing room; avoid pure white on screens

### Gray
**Associations**: Balance, neutrality, professionalism, maturity
**Industries**: Technology, finance, professional services
**Variations**:
- Warm gray: Approachable
- Cool gray: Modern, tech-forward

### Brown
**Associations**: Earthiness, reliability, warmth, organic, heritage
**Industries**: Food, outdoor, artisanal, coffee, legal

## Industry-Specific Guidance

### Technology/SaaS
- Primary: Blue, purple, or bold accent colors
- Avoid: Brown, overly traditional palettes
- Trend: Gradients, vibrant accent colors

### Finance/Legal
- Primary: Blue, green, black
- Avoid: Bright playful colors
- Goal: Trust and stability

### Health/Wellness
- Primary: Green, blue, white
- Secondary: Soft pastels, earth tones
- Goal: Calm and trustworthy

### Food & Beverage
- Primary: Red, orange, yellow, green
- Avoid: Blue (suppresses appetite, except for seafood)
- Goal: Appetite appeal

### Creative/Agency
- Primary: Bold, unexpected combinations
- Freedom: More experimental palettes accepted
- Goal: Creativity and differentiation

### E-commerce/Retail
- Primary: Depends on positioning
- Discount: Red, yellow, orange
- Premium: Black, white, gold

## Color Combination Strategies

### Monochromatic
Single color in varying shades. Safe, cohesive, can lack energy.

### Complementary
Opposite colors on wheel (blue/orange). High contrast, dynamic.

### Analogous
Adjacent colors (blue/green/teal). Harmonious, natural feeling.

### Triadic
Three colors equally spaced. Vibrant, balanced when one dominates.

## Accessibility Considerations

- Ensure sufficient contrast (WCAG AA: 4.5:1 for text)
- Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning
- Test for color blindness (8% of men, 0.5% of women)
- Provide monochrome version for all logos
